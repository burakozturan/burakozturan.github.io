Social media platforms like Twitter/X have the potential to empower ordinary individuals and marginalized groups to participate in democratic deliberation. However, in practice, the information ecosystem is highly concentrated, with a small group of users and domains dominating the landscape. This study defines the extent of this concentration across various topics and platform affordances and examines the demographics of users who dominate the ecosystem.

The findings highlight significant concentration within both the political and general information ecosystems on Twitter at the user and domain levels. Political discussions are particularly concentrated, with a small number of users and domains exerting substantial influence, underscoring the dominance of a few voices in this space. Platform affordances further amplify the reach of these highly effective users, magnifying their influence.

Demographic analysis reveals the impact of gender and age on visibility and influence within political discourse. Despite being more active in sharing political content, females are less effective in generating attention compared to males, highlighting a gender gap in the ability to influence discussions despite higher levels of participation.

Understanding these dynamics is crucial to addressing disparities in influence and improving the equality of information dissemination on social media platforms.
